What are Gas Giants?

Gas giants are large planets that are primarily composed of hydrogen and helium. Unlike rocky planets, gas giants do not have a well-defined solid surface. The most well-known gas giants in our solar system are Jupiter and Saturn.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system and is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for centuries.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning ring system, which is made up of ice and rock particles. It is the second-largest planet in our solar system.
