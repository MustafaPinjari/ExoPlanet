Finding Exoplanets

Discover the methods used to find planets beyond our solar system

Transit Method

The transit method is one of the most successful techniques for discovering exoplanets. It involves monitoring the brightness of a star over time. When a planet passes in front of the star, it causes a temporary dip in the star's brightness, indicating the presence of a planet.

How It Works

As a planet transits its host star, it blocks a small fraction of the star's light, causing a dip in brightness that can be detected by telescopes.

Light Curves

The light curve of a star shows the changes in brightness over time. A periodic dip in the light curve indicates the presence of a transiting planet.

Advantages of the Transit Method

  • Allows for the determination of the planet's size.
  • Can be used to study the planet's atmosphere through transmission spectroscopy.
  • Enables the detection of multiple planets in the same system.
  • Provides precise measurements of the planet's orbital period.
